About Me

I am a recent graduate with a passion for cybersecurity, data analysis, and teaching. I am looking for a job in the IT field that will use a combinations of all of these passions. After taking my first Informatics class I immediately knew I wanted to make Information Technology my career and hobby. Information Technology encompasses such a wide array and cybersecurity caught my attention due to the ever changing and growing threats that all industries face. I wanted to make a difference by protecting assets while also facing challenging situations, whether it be doing digital forensics or drafting policies that protect organizations. I have a interest in cybersecurity as it is an ever evolving field that will always keep me on my toes as new exploits and vulnerabilities are found every day. I also have taken data visualization courses that have taught me the value of working with data firsthand and the immediate impact intuitive dashboards can have on decision making. I have been able to create visualization and figure out why certain relationships exist between seemingly unrelated pieces of data to reveal the bigger picture. While data analysis is a field that interests me on its own, its combination with areas like cybersecurity has the potential to unveil groundbreaking insights and provide educational opportunities. Education is the other field that I have been involved and passionate in for over two years in computational mathematical foundations. I was able to communicate the passion I have for Information Technology and help a wide range of students from varied, diverse backgrounds. It helped me become a more confident public speaker and educator as I taught in front of over one hundred students throughout my tenure as a lead undergraduate instructor. In this course, I have instructed students in comprehending computer computations and developing their own code. Introducing new topics to students has been inspiring as I guide them through new challenges and see each person grow their skills.
